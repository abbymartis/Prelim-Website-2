Technology Risks

With the advancement of technology, social media platforms have weaved themselves into the fabric of daily living, providing new avenues for entertainment, communication, and sharing vital information. Cybercriminals who engage in identity deception, phishing schemes, and other forms of online frauds also find social networks to be particularly appealing targets. The threat remains progressively sophisticated, and users become increasingly vulnerable in the face of considerable personal and monetary risks. Studies indicate the necessity for both awareness and education as means of lessening the susceptibility of the user from such frauds (Mouncey & Ciobotaru, 2025).

One of the most serious issues on social networking sites is identity theft, whereby malicious individuals hack and take personal data about a person and pretend to be the person themselves. Social networking customers share sensitive information like birthdates, whereabouts, and who they are related to without realizing they are providing attackers with crucial information used to guess passwords or answer security queries. Fake accounts and impersonation additionally complicate the matter on top of this, making scammers post whatever they desire from a victimâ€™s network. Malicious third-party apps and quizzes asking customers for data access and harvesting it for fraudulent activity also present a rising threat.

Phishing scams on social networking sites also abound. Attackers also use misleading messages, imitation brand pages, or bogus giveaways as a way of luring victims into clicking on malicious links or divulging sensitive information. As stated by Mouncey and Ciobotaru (2025), social media apps like Instagram become targeted as well for the scams. Phishing messages also look legitimate and originate from trusted sources like known friends or recognized brands. The moment a user falls for the scam, their account might be compromised and used for further propagation of the attack and thus enhance both the attack's coverage and total effect.

One of the key reasons why phishing scams succeed is simply the general unawareness of the average user. Mouncey and Ciobotaru (2025) established that most users lack the understanding of what constitutes or how they should manage a phishing attack. Even where there is available cyber awareness training, it may be hampered by time issues, lack of interest, or feelings of irrelevance. This underlines the necessity for better interactive and accessible educational methods.

These challenges can be mitigated by several strategies proposed by Mouncey and Ciobotaru (2025). A good approach is employing digital nudges and micro-modules of training which present brief and specific cybersecurity advice when individuals make normal usage of social media use. Bite-sized interventions tend to be learned and utilized by the individuals better than dense advice. The study also recommends imposing mandatory cybersecurity training programs in schools, workplaces as well as government institutions. By institutionalizing cybersecurity awareness and making the training interactive, the individuals are better prepared to identify and resist the threat posed by phishing attacks.

Furthermore, identity theft and phishing attacks on social media present serious digital challenges in our interconnected society. Because cybercriminals evolve and change continually, so must the educational efforts at prevention. Building awareness through interactive and accessible training can minimize susceptibility and safeguard personal data. Eventually, every effort at developing a culture of digital responsibility will be necessary to sustain security within the ever-changing platform of social media.
